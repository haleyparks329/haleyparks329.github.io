The homepage is organized as a desk because the work is easier to understand as objects in progress than as a polished marketing funnel.

A tidy portfolio can make every project feel equally finished. That is not honest for this site. Some things are working systems. Some are experiments. Some are notes from the edge of the work. Some are questions that have not earned a clean answer yet.

The desk metaphor helps because it can hold different levels of certainty at once. A project report can sit beside a field note. A replay can sit beside a limitation. A public artifact can sit beside the question that produced it.

The useful feeling is not clutter. It is inspectability. A good public work surface can feel like someone stepped away for a minute and left the useful artifacts where they can be read.

## Still thinking

The metaphor should support the content, not become the content. If the desk makes the work harder to read, the design is doing the wrong job.
