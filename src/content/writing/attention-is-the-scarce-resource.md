## The claim

Attention is the scarce resource. Useful personal AI should reduce the amount a person has to remember, coordinate, search, and connect for themselves without hiding uncertainty or removing judgment.

## What prompted this

This idea came out of the same pressure that keeps showing up across my projects. The Human Model is partly about remembering context across training, health, movement, and conversation. Career Intelligence is partly about keeping career evidence connected to the claims it supports. QA Agents is partly about preserving the evidence behind a software-quality recommendation.

Different projects, same problem: the person should not have to hold the whole system in their head.

## Why the current approach fails

A lot of software gives people more places to check. More dashboards, more notifications, more generated summaries, more places where context might live.

That can look helpful while still increasing the coordination burden. The person still has to remember which system knows what, where the current truth lives, what changed, and what still needs judgment.

## What I think instead

The better role for software is to carry more of the connective burden. It should remember what happened, connect related context, preserve evidence, and make uncertainty visible.

That does not mean the system should decide everything. It means the person should spend less attention on reconstructing context and more attention on the decision that actually needs them.

## Evidence and examples

In The Human Model, a workout is not useful only as load, reps, and effort. It becomes more useful when it can be reviewed alongside recovery, soreness, recent history, and the notes that explain what was happening around the session.

In QA Agents, a passing test run is not the whole story. The useful part is the evidence trail: what changed, what passed, what remained uncovered, and why a human should review the next step.

In Career Intelligence, generated writing is safer when it stays attached to source-backed evidence. The system should help preserve context rather than produce confident language detached from proof.

## Where this argument is incomplete

I do not think every cognitive burden should be automated away. Some work is how a person understands the thing they are doing.

The open question is where support becomes substitution. A system that gives attention back is useful. A system that hides the parts that still require judgment is dangerous.

## Conclusion

The test I keep returning to is simple: does this system give attention back, or does it create another surface that needs to be managed?
